Getting to the know the user

Ever since I was a kid Iâ€™ve had a considerable curiosity about people and their lives. I love learning about what people do for work and why, how they tick, what makes them excited/frustrated, how their hometown/life experiences shaped who they are today, their goals and aspirations.

In a user research context I believe that creating a more holistic model of the user generally leads to alternative models to think of the problem at hand and more opporunities for creative design solutions. Some methods I use:

User Interviews: With a list of prepared questions and target information I want to find out, my interview approach is to make the user feel comfortable and confident with sharing their viewpoints and information about the product.

Contextual Inquiry: What better way to learn what a user does for work then actually going their workplace and seeing for yourself? In the past Iâ€™ve visited emergency rooms to observe charge nurses and doctors, fire department stations, makeup events, and gone out as a volunteer to serve the Harvard Square homeless population.

Usability/Hypothesis Testing: As an experimenter and participant, much of my academic experience has taught me how to conduct quality experiment design to justify assumptions or uncover unexpected outcomes. I try to come up with a testable hypothesis for what works/doesn't work about a digital product and design an experiment to test the hypothesis.
