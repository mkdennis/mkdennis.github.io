About Me

Hi I'm Dennis. During my time at university, I've had the opportunity to study a wide range of topics including business, international affairs, philosophy, but most of my time has been spent studying the intersection between design, cognitive psychology, and computer science.

Interdisciplinary by nature (I'm usually into a million different things at any given time) my approach to designing products involves connecting the dots between multiple perspectives and solutions. I love having indepth conversations with users and stakeholders to get to the nitty gritty of the problem at hand.

The design principles I use are derived from books and articles I've read, most notably: About Face by Alan Cooper, Don't Make Me Think by Steve Kruger, Julie Zhuo's Medium posts, and the lessons over at Hackdesign.org.

Outside of the classroom, I'm either designing layouts for the Observer, building my school's Korean student community, and most recently learning how to DJ. Thanks for dropping by!
